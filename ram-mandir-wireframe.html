About the Teerth Kshetra

A Mandir Shaped by Vastu & Devotion

श्री राम जन्मभूमि तीर्थ क्षेत्र श्री राम जन्मभूमि मन्दिर को वास्तु शास्त्र के अनुसार भव्य स्वरूप प्रदान कराने हेतु कार्यरत है।

The Trust works to give the Shri Ram Janmbhoomi Mandir a grand form in accordance with Vastu Shastra, and to make the surrounding complex secure and comfortable for every devotee who comes for darshan.

Built in the Nagara style of temple architecture in pink Bansi Paharpur sandstone, the Mandir rises through carved tiers to a gilded kalash — a continuity of a tradition many centuries old, raised with full transparency.

Janma Tithi · The Birth of Shri Ram

When Was Shri Ram Born?

Tradition fixes his birth on Ram Navami; modern researchers have read the planetary positions Valmiki recorded to propose an astronomical date. Both are presented here side by side.

The Traditional Date

Chaitra Shukla Navami

Shri Ram was born at noon on the ninth day of the bright fortnight of Chaitra, under the Punarvasu nakshatra — observed across India as Ram Navami, in March or April each year.

The Astronomical Reading

10 January 5114 BCE

Researcher Pushkar Bhatnagar fed the sky Valmiki describes in the Bala Kanda into planetarium software. Viewed from Ayodhya (25°N, 81°E), the configuration matched midday on this date — which also falls on Chaitra Shukla Navami.

Hanuman Chalisa · The Distance to the Sun

जुग सहस्र जोजन पर भानू।
लील्यो ताहि मधुर फल जानू॥

"Juga sahasra jojana para bhānū, līlyo tāhi madhura phala jānū" — the infant Hanuman leapt toward the Sun, thinking it a sweet ripe fruit.

Devotees read the opening words as a distance: Yug × Sahasra × Yojan. With the traditional values, the figure lands remarkably close to the true Earth–Sun distance.

12,0001 Yug × 1,000Sahasra × 8 mi1 Yojan = 96,000,000miles

≈ 96 million miles — strikingly near the measured mean distance to the Sun, about 93 million miles (149.6 million km).

Astronomical datings of this kind are debated: such planetary patterns recur over long cycles, and the value of a yojana varies between texts (roughly 3.5–15 km). These readings are shared as fascinating interpretation, not settled science. The living observance remains Ram Navami.

The Ramayanas

One Story, Many Sacred Tongues

From Maharshi Valmiki's original Sanskrit epic to Goswami Tulsidas's beloved Awadhi retelling, the life of Shri Ram has been sung across India and beyond for millennia.

वाल्मीकि रामायणValmiki Ramayana
Sanskrit · The Adi Kavya

The first epic poem ever composed, by Maharshi Valmiki, the Adi Kavi. Around 24,000 shlokas across seven kandas — Bala, Ayodhya, Aranya, Kishkindha, Sundara, Yuddha and Uttara — narrating Shri Ram's life in its original form.

श्रीरामचरितमानसTulsidas Ramcharitmanas
Awadhi · 16th Century

Composed by Goswami Tulsidas, begun on Ram Navami in 1574 CE. Written in Awadhi so that every devotee could sing of Ram, it remains the most cherished retelling across North India — also the source of the Hanuman Chalisa.

अध्यात्म रामायणAdhyatma Ramayana
Sanskrit · Devotional

A philosophical retelling that reveals Shri Ram as the Supreme Brahman, blending the path of devotion with Advaita wisdom. It deeply influenced later vernacular Ramayanas.

…and across many lands and languages

The Ramayana tradition flowered in regional and distant tongues — each a treasure of its people:

The Birth of the Adi Kavya

From Grief, the First Verse

By the banks of the river Tamasa, the sage Valmiki watched a pair of krauncha cranes circling in love. In an instant a hunter's arrow felled the male, and the surviving bird's cry of anguish pierced the sage's heart.

Overcome, Valmiki spoke — and his sorrow poured out not as plain speech but in perfect metre. That spontaneous utterance became the very first shloka, and poetry itself was born from compassion.

Soon after, the celestial sage Narada and Lord Brahma inspired Valmiki to turn that same metre to a far greater purpose: to sing the whole life of Shri Ram. So arose the Ramayana — the Adi Kavya, the first epic — in some 24,000 shlokas across seven kandas.

शोक→श्लोक grief became verse
The First Shloka

मा निषाद प्रतिष्ठां त्वमगमः शाश्वतीः समाः।
यत्क्रौञ्चमिथुनादेकमवधीः काममोहितम्॥

"mā niṣāda pratiṣṭhāṃ tvam agamaḥ śāśvatīḥ samāḥ…"

"O hunter, may you find no peace through the long years — for you have slain one of a pair of krauncha birds lost in love." — the spontaneous lament that became the world's first metrical verse.

Shri Ram Janmabhoomi

The Long Journey Home

A timeline of the movement and the legal path that led to the rebuilding of the Mandir at the birthplace of Shri Ram.

1528

A structure is built at the site, traditionally believed to be the birthplace of Lord Ram.

1853–1859

The earliest recorded clashes near the site; a fence is later erected to allow separate access to both communities.

1885

A petition by Mahant Raghubir Das seeking permission to build a canopy near the structure is rejected by the Faizabad court.

1949

Idols of Ram and Sita are placed inside the site, which the government then declares disputed.

1984 – 1989

A committee is formed to build a temple, and in 1989 the Vishwa Hindu Parishad lays the foundation (Shilanyas).

1992

The disputed structure is demolished on 6 December; the Liberhan Commission is later set up to inquire into the events.

2010

The Allahabad High Court rules to divide the site three ways among the Sunni Waqf Board, Nirmohi Akhara, and Ram Lalla.

2011 – 2017

The Supreme Court stays the High Court verdict and later calls for an out-of-court settlement among the parties.

9 Nov 2019

After 40 days of day-to-day hearings, the Supreme Court awards the site for the Mandir and directs five acres of alternate land for a mosque.

Feb 2020

The Shri Ram Janmabhoomi Teerth Kshetra Trust is announced to oversee construction of the Mandir.

5 Aug 2020

The Bhumi Pujan is performed and the foundation stone laid, marking the formal start of construction.

22 Jan 2024

The Pran Pratishtha — the consecration of Ram Lalla in the sanctum — is performed, and the Mandir opens to devotees.

The Mandir · Architecture

A Masterwork in the Nagara Tradition

Designed by the Sompura family of temple architects and built without a single piece of iron, the Mandir rises in three storeys of carved pink sandstone — a structure intended to stand for a thousand years.

Form & Vision

Carved to Endure

The Mandir spans a built-up area of about 57,400 sq ft across three floors of twenty feet each, rising to a shikhara 161 feet high. Larsen & Toubro built it at no cost, with Tata Consulting Engineers as project consultant.

India's foremost institutions — the CBRI, NGRI, and IITs at Bombay, Guwahati and Madras — guided the soil, concrete and structural engineering. A 21-foot granite plinth guards against ground moisture, and no iron is used anywhere in the structure.

Within the Complex

  • Five Mandaps: Nritya, Rang, Sabha, Prarthna and Kirtan Mandap.
  • Parkota: a rectangular compound wall, 732 m long and 14 ft wide, surrounds the temple.
  • Four corner temples: Surya Dev, Devi Bhagwati, Ganesh Bhagwan and Bhagwan Shiv.
  • Northern & southern arms: Maa Annapurna and Lord Hanuman.
  • Proposed sage temples: Maharshis Valmiki, Vashishtha, Vishwamitra and Agastya, with Nishad Raj, Mata Shabri and Devi Ahilya.
  • Kuber Tila: an ancient Shiv temple restored, with a statue of Jatayu installed.
  • 21-ft granite plinth protects the structure against ground moisture.
  • No iron is used anywhere in the Mandir's construction.

Ram Bhakti Parampara

Saints & Devotees of Shri Ram

Across centuries, India's greatest poets, sages and saints have walked the path of Ram Naam — leaving behind a treasury of devotion that still lights the way.

हनु

Shri Hanuman

The Supreme Devotee

Ram bhakt par excellence — his devotion is the very ideal of bhakti. Wherever the name of Ram is sung, Hanuman is said to be present.

वा

Maharshi Valmiki

The Adi Kavi

The first poet, who received and composed the original Ramayana — giving the world the life of Shri Ram in its earliest form.

तु

Goswami Tulsidas

Poet-Saint, 16th c.

Author of the Ramcharitmanas and the Hanuman Chalisa, he brought the story of Ram into the language and heart of the common devotee.

रा

Samarth Ramdas

Saint & Guru, 17th c.

Author of the Dasbodh and guru to Chhatrapati Shivaji, he established many Ram and Hanuman temples and spread the strength of devotion.

त्या

Sant Tyagaraja

Composer-Saint

One of the greatest composers of Carnatic music, whose countless kritis are outpourings of pure love for Shri Ram.

भ

Bhadrachala Ramadasu

Devotee-Builder, 17th c.

Kancherla Gopanna, who built the celebrated Ram temple at Bhadrachalam and sang of Ram through years of hardship.

Prabhu Ki Kripa

Miracles & the Grace of God

Cherished accounts from scripture and living tradition — held dear by devotees as testaments to the boundless compassion of Shri Ram.

शबरी के बेर

The Devotion of Shabari

Tradition recounts how Shri Ram, moved by the pure heart of the tribal devotee Shabari, lovingly accepted the berries she offered — teaching that the Lord looks only at the devotion behind the gift.

केवट प्रसंग

Kevat, the Boatman

As remembered in the Ramayana, the humble boatman Kevat washed Shri Ram's feet before ferrying him across the Ganga — his simple faith honoured above all riches.

अहल्या उद्धार

The Redemption of Ahalya

Scripture tells that the mere touch of Shri Ram's feet restored Ahalya from stone to life — a beloved symbol of grace that lifts the fallen and forgives.

राम नाम की शक्ति

The Power of Ram Naam

Saints across the ages have held that the very name of Ram carries the Lord's grace — a refuge offered freely to anyone who calls upon it with a sincere heart.

These narratives are shared as part of devotional and scriptural heritage, in the spirit of faith and reverence in which they have been cherished for generations.

Uttar Pradesh

2

Near Prayagraj

3

Chitrakoot

UP – MP border

Ram dwells in the forest here; Bharat comes to plead his return and bears his sandals to the throne.

4

Panchavati

Nashik, Maharashtra

In the Dandaka forest, Sita is abducted by Ravana — and the great search for her begins.

5

Kishkindha

Hampi, Karnataka

Across the sea
